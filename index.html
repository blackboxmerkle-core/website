Who We Are

Trust without opacity.

Black Box Merkle is a Scottish technology company focused on building open, verifiable cryptographic infrastructure. We believe that data integrity shouldn't require blind trust — it should be mathematically provable.

Named for the intersection of cryptographic Merkle trees and the principle of transparent, auditable systems, we specialise in tooling that makes complex cryptographic primitives accessible to builders.

Based in Edinburgh, we work at the frontier of verifiable computation, decentralised data structures, and zero-knowledge proof systems.

What We Build

Cryptographic tooling
for the modern web.

01
Merkle Tree Systems

Efficient construction, verification, and management of Merkle trees for data integrity, state commitments, and cryptographic audit trails.

02
Zero-Knowledge Proofs

Design and implementation of ZK circuits and proof systems enabling selective disclosure and privacy-preserving verification without revealing underlying data.

03
Verifiable Data Structures

Authenticated data structures for distributed systems — sparse Merkle trees, accumulators, and commitment schemes for scalable, trustless applications.

04
Smart Contract Auditing

Formal verification and cryptographic auditing of smart contracts, with specialisation in Merkle proof verification, hash function usage, and access control.

05
Proof-of-Existence

Timestamp and anchor document hashes on-chain with cryptographic guarantees. Immutable proof that data existed at a specific point in time.

06
Integrity Consulting

Architecture review and consultancy for teams building on cryptographic primitives. We help you choose the right tools and avoid costly design mistakes.

Why It Matters

The case for
verifiable trust.

01
Data integrity at scale

Merkle trees enable O(log n) verification of data membership across billions of records without centralised authorities.

02
Privacy-preserving proofs

Zero-knowledge proofs let you prove you know something without revealing what it is — the foundation of private, verifiable systems.

03
Auditability without surveillance

Cryptographic commitments enable public verifiability of state transitions without exposing sensitive underlying data.

04
Open-source first

Security through transparency. Our tooling is publicly auditable because trust should be earned, not assumed.
